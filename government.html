Structure of the Aeretocracy

The Sangaian government is built on the principle that governance should be as immutable as geography. The Aeretocracy (Rule of Virtue) ensures that power is not centralized in a single fluctuating leader, but distributed through a rigid framework of constitutional ethics.

Unlike representative democracies where policies shift with election cycles, Sangaia relies on the people and their representative Body of Electives to interpret the original intent of our founding laws.

Key Principles

  • Non-Intervention: We respect the sovereignty of native tribes and bordering nations.
  • Constitutional Supremacy: No executive order can bypass the law of the Constitution.
  • Transparency: Any and all provincial budgets are published for citizen review.

The Aeretocrat's Role

The Aeretocrat serves as the symbolic head and chief arbitrator. However, their power is strictly limited to enforcement rather than creation of law. This prevents the rise of authoritarianism while ensuring swift justice.
